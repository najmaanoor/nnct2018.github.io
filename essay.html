Essay

Your personal essay is your first introduction to the college you're applying too. Here are some quick tips on how to easliy write an organized essay.



Analyze the prompt
Take time to think about the prompt
Divide the prompt into phrases, look at each aspect
Think of how the information relates to your ability to excel in college


Organize your writing
Create an outline
Think about how long each paragraph should be
Consider the word count limits
Figure out when you’re going to write
Create a schedule


SHOW (don’t tell)
Admissions officers are more interested in your perspective of what happened than the events themselves
Keep in mind: admissions officers don’t know you personally
Write your essay so the reader can feel as if they’re experiencing the situation alongside you
Describe what you learned from the experience and how it changed you




No contradiction or repetition
Your essay should not contradict any other part of your application
It should not be a repeat of your application
Don’t list your awards or discuss your grades or test scores


Write sophisticatedly (know your vocab)
Reflect a college-level vocabulary
Substitute with an advanced vocabulary word
Look online for examples of that word in action to avoid misuse of vocabulary
Majority of the sentences in your essay should be compound, complex, or compound-complex sentences
Avoid overdoing it (advanced vocabulary should be used to spice the essay)
Be careful when trying to be funny (some humor can be considered unproffesional to adults)




Get advice from qualified people before submission
Ask a teacher or college counselor
Turn to individuals who have background and expertise in the college admissions process
Limit the number of people you ask to two or three to reduce confusion when revising your essay
Before submitting, triple check to make sure your essay is free of spelling or grammar errors
